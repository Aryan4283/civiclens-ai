# Municipal Service Level Agreement (SLA) Document
## Urban Local Body — Infrastructure & Public Services
### Effective Date: April 1, 2024 | Document Version: 3.2

---

> This document defines the mandatory service level commitments, response timelines, responsible departments, and escalation procedures for all civic infrastructure issues reported by citizens within the municipal jurisdiction.

---

## PART A: ROAD INFRASTRUCTURE

### Section 3.1 — Potholes and Road Surface Defects

**Responsible Department:** Public Works Department (PWD)

**Classification and Response Timelines:**

**Class A — Critical Defects:**
- Definition: Potholes or depressions exceeding 30 cm in diameter OR 10 cm in depth, located on primary arterial roads, national highways, or state highways within city limits.
- Risk Level: HIGH — poses immediate risk to vehicles and two-wheelers; potential for accidents.
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **72 hours from time of reporting.**
- Interim Action Required: Safety barricade or warning sign must be placed within 6 hours of report acknowledgement.
- Cited Rule: "Section 3.1(a) — Class A road defects must be permanently repaired within 72 hours. Temporary patching is not acceptable for Class A defects on arterial roads."

**Class B — Moderate Defects:**
- Definition: Potholes between 15–30 cm diameter on secondary roads, residential streets, or service roads.
- Risk Level: MEDIUM
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **7 calendar days from time of reporting.**
- Cited Rule: "Section 3.1(b) — Class B defects on secondary and residential roads must be addressed within 7 days. Emergency patching is acceptable as first response, followed by permanent repair."

**Class C — Minor Defects:**
- Definition: Surface cracks, minor depressions less than 15 cm diameter, pavement wear without safety risk.
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **30 calendar days.**
- Cited Rule: "Section 3.1(c) — Minor road defects are to be included in the next scheduled maintenance cycle, not exceeding 30 days."

**Escalation Authority:** District Collector (Roads Division) / Municipal Commissioner

---

### Section 3.2 — Road Damage and Infrastructure

**Responsible Department:** Public Works Department (PWD)

- Broken road dividers on arterial roads: **48 hours**
- Damaged footpaths obstructing pedestrian movement: **5 calendar days**
- Missing manhole covers on roads (HIGH DANGER): **24 hours — treated as Class A emergency**
- Damaged speed breakers posing safety risk: **72 hours**

Cited Rule: "Section 3.2 — All road infrastructure damage that creates an immediate public safety hazard is classified as Priority 1 and must be addressed within 24–72 hours regardless of road category."

## PART B: WATER SUPPLY AND DRAINAGE

### Section 4.1 — Water Supply Issues

**Responsible Department:** Municipal Water Supply Board / Jal Board

**Active Water Leakage (Burst Pipes):**
- Definition: Visible water gushing, pooling, or flowing continuously from supply infrastructure.
- Risk Level: CRITICAL — water waste, road damage, potential contamination.
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **24 hours from report.**
- Interim action: Water supply to affected section may be temporarily shut off within 4 hours.
- Cited Rule: "Section 4.1(a) — Active water leakages from supply mains or distribution pipes constitute a Priority 1 emergency. The field crew must be dispatched within 2 hours of complaint registration. Full repair must be completed within 24 hours."

**Minor Leakage (Seepage, Dripping):**
- Definition: Slow drips, minor seepage from joints or connections, non-flowing leaks.
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **72 hours.**
- Cited Rule: "Section 4.1(b) — Minor seepage and slow leaks must be repaired within 72 hours to prevent escalation to burst pipe status."

**Water Supply Disruption (No Water / Low Pressure):**
- For more than 6 hours in a residential area: **Resolution or explanation within 12 hours.**
- For more than 24 hours: **Mandatory tanker supply within 6 hours of 24-hour mark.**
- Cited Rule: "Section 4.1(c) — Citizens have a right to uninterrupted water supply. Supply disruptions exceeding 24 hours require both immediate interim relief (tanker) and root cause repair within 48 hours."

**Escalation Authority:** Chief Engineer, Water Supply / Municipal Commissioner

---

### Section 4.2 — Drainage and Flooding

**Responsible Department:** Municipal Storm Water Drainage Department

**Blocked Drains (Non-urgent):**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **5 calendar days**
- Cited Rule: "Section 4.2(a) — Blocked or choked drainage channels in residential areas must be cleared within 5 days."

**Overflowing Drains / Active Flooding:**
- Definition: Drainage overflow causing water accumulation on roads or entering residential premises.
- Risk Level: HIGH — disease risk, property damage, traffic disruption.
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **24 hours.**
- Cited Rule: "Section 4.2(b) — Active drain overflow causing road flooding is a Priority 1 issue. Pumping crew must respond within 4 hours. Full clearance within 24 hours."

**Damaged Drain Cover / Open Drain Hazard:**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **48 hours** — treated as safety emergency.
- Cited Rule: "Section 4.2(c) — Exposed or damaged drain covers posing fall hazard must be covered or barricaded within 4 hours and permanently repaired within 48 hours."

## PART C: ELECTRICAL AND STREET LIGHTING

### Section 5.1 — Street Light Failures

**Responsible Department:** Municipal Electricity Department / State Electricity Distribution Company

**Single Street Light Failure:**
- Definition: One or two adjacent lights not functioning on a residential or secondary street.
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **72 hours.**
- Cited Rule: "Section 5.1(a) — Individual street light failures must be repaired within 72 hours of reporting. Replacement of bulbs, ballasts, or control gear falls under routine maintenance."

**Multiple Street Light Failure (3+ lights / entire stretch):**
- Definition: Complete darkness on a road section due to multiple light failures or feeder failure.
- Risk Level: HIGH — crime risk, accident risk.
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **48 hours.**
- Cited Rule: "Section 5.1(b) — Multiple street light failure on any road stretch is classified as Priority 2. Temporary lighting arrangements must be made within 24 hours if full repair is not possible."

**Main Road / Highway Lighting Failure:**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **24 hours.**
- Cited Rule: "Section 5.1(c) — Street lighting failure on arterial roads, national/state highways, and high-traffic corridors must be treated as Priority 1 with 24-hour resolution mandate."

**Damaged Light Poles (Accident Risk):**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **24 hours.** Treated as safety emergency.

**Escalation Authority:** Superintending Engineer, Electrical / Chief Electrical Officer

## PART D: WASTE MANAGEMENT AND SANITATION

### Section 6.1 — Garbage and Solid Waste

**Responsible Department:** Municipal Sanitation Department / Solid Waste Management Cell

**Missed Garbage Collection (Residential):**
- Mandatory Redressal: Collection within **24 hours** of complaint.
- Cited Rule: "Section 6.1(a) — Scheduled garbage collection must not be missed for more than 24 hours. Complaints regarding missed pickup must result in collection by the next working day."

**Illegal Dumping / Large Garbage Accumulation:**
- Definition: Waste accumulated on public land, road margins, or community spaces.
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **48 hours.**
- Cited Rule: "Section 6.1(b) — Illegal dumping sites and large accumulations of solid waste in public areas must be cleared within 48 hours. Action against violators must be initiated within 7 days."

**Overflowing Public Bins:**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **12 hours.**
- Cited Rule: "Section 6.1(c) — Overflowing public garbage bins are a sanitation hazard and must be emptied within 12 hours of report."

**Dead Animal Removal:**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **6 hours** — health hazard.

**Escalation Authority:** Health Officer / Chief Sanitary Inspector

---

### Section 6.2 — Open Defecation and Sanitation Facilities

**Broken Public Toilet Facilities:**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **72 hours.**
- If only facility in area: Portable alternative within **24 hours.**

## PART E: PUBLIC PROPERTY AND INFRASTRUCTURE

### Section 7.1 — Public Parks and Open Spaces

**Damaged Park Equipment (Safety Hazard):**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **48 hours.**

**Overgrown Vegetation Blocking Visibility / Road:**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **72 hours** for safety-critical locations, **15 days** otherwise.

---

### Section 7.2 — Government Buildings and Public Amenities

**Damaged Bus Stop Shelter:**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **7 calendar days.**

**Broken Public Bench or Seating:**
- Mandatory Resolution Time: **15 calendar days.**

## PART F: ESCALATION PROCEDURES

### Section 8.1 — Standard Escalation Chain

When a department fails to resolve an issue within the mandated SLA timeline, the following escalation chain is automatically activated:

**Level 1 (SLA Breach — Day 1 after deadline):**
- Auto-notification sent to Department Head / Executive Engineer.
- Issue flagged as "ESCALATED" in the civic portal.
- Cited Rule: "Section 8.1(a) — Any complaint unresolved beyond its SLA deadline must be automatically escalated to the Department Head within 24 hours of breach."

**Level 2 (3 days after SLA breach):**
- Escalation to Municipal Commissioner / City CEO.
- Formal notice issued to department.
- Cited Rule: "Section 8.1(b) — Complaints remaining unresolved 3 days after SLA breach are escalated to the Municipal Commissioner with a mandatory written explanation from the department within 48 hours."

**Level 3 (7 days after SLA breach):**
- Escalation to District Collector.
- Matter treated as administrative failure.
- Cited Rule: "Section 8.1(c) — Persistent non-resolution beyond 7 days of SLA breach constitutes administrative negligence. District Collector shall initiate inquiry under Municipal Act provisions."

---

### Section 8.2 — Priority Classification Summary

| Priority | Response Time | Applies To |
|----------|--------------|------------|
| P0 — Emergency | 2–6 hours | Missing manholes, burst mains, fallen poles |
| P1 — Critical | 24 hours | Active flooding, main road darkness, dead animals |
| P2 — High | 48–72 hours | Class A potholes, multiple streetlights, active leaks |
| P3 — Medium | 5–7 days | Class B potholes, blocked drains, illegal dumping |
| P4 — Routine | 15–30 days | Class C defects, minor amenity repairs |

---

### Section 8.3 — Citizen Rights

1. Every reported complaint must receive an acknowledgement within 2 hours.
2. Citizens have the right to track their complaint status in real time.
3. If a complaint is rejected, the reason must be communicated within 24 hours.
4. Citizens may re-escalate any complaint closed without satisfactory resolution within 7 days.
5. Anonymous reporting is permitted for all public space issues.
